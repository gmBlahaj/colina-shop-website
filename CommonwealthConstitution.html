1. Rights of every Commonwealth citizen (CWR)

 

§1: Protection of Honor

(1) The honor of every citizen of the Commonwealth is not able to be violated.

 

§2: Free Expression of Diversity

(1) Every Citizen has the right to freely show their personality, as long as the rights of other     citizens aren't infringed upon.

(2) Every Citizen has the right to live without being harmed by others. Only Executive Orders or Martial Law is able to bypass this.

 

§3:  Judicial equality for every Citizen.

(1) Every Citizen is equal in court.

(2) Nobody is allowed to be discriminated based on:

  1. Race

  2. Religion

  3. Sexuality

  4. Political beliefs

 

§4: Freedom of Expression

(1) Every Citizen has the right to express their opinion in text, word or picture and to share their opinions.

(2) However this is limited by following laws prohibiting harrasment or discrimination.

 

§5:  Freedom of Assembly

(1) Every Citizen has the right to assemble in groups, without drawn weapons.

(2) Limitations can be enforced when in the open.

 

§6: Freedom of Protest

(1) Every Citizen has the right to protest actions of their government, as long as it stays peaceful.

(2) If violence occurs, the assembly will be declared as illegal and dissidents will be tried.

 

 

 

§7: Unobstructed housing

(1) The house of Citizens is not allowed to be tampered with.

(2) Searches are only to be conducted by certified personnel after the order by a judge.

 

§8: Right to ownership of goods

(1) Citizens are entitled to the ownership of legally aquired goods.

(2) Illegaly aquired goods do not fall under the ownership of their current holder and can be reaquired by their legal owner.

 

§9: Right to political asylum:

(1) Foreigners have the right to apply for political asylum if a valid reason has bee provided.

(2) These Reasons include: Civil War, Extreme governmental strive or the escape from a dictatorship.

 

§10: Right to pass petitions

(1) Citizens possess the right to pass around petitions which demand an action from the  ill government.

(2) If a 60% approval rate has been achieved, the government is required to perform this action.

 

§11: Removal of rights

(1) If a Citizen is found to abuse any of the above mentioned rights, he is able to have rights removed via judicial orders.

 

§12: Right to resistance

All Citizens have the right:

(1) to resist the government if it tries to abolish democracy if no other way is available.

(2) to resist the government when unjust actions are performed.

(3) to resist when their power is being abused.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

2. Election in the Commonwealth (CWE)

 

§1: Basic elections

(1) Elections are to be performed anonymously.

(2) Elections are not to be tampered with by current government officials.

(3) Parties are to be elected, not persons.

(4) Current government officials must hand over their duty to their successors if beat.

 

§2: Election timeframe

(1) Elections are to be held in a fixed timeframe.

(2) This timeframe shall not be changed after the start of the vote.

(3) This timeframe shall not exceed 24 hours/1 day.

 

 

3. The Rules of Parliament (CWP)

§1: The Prime Minister

(1) The Prime Minister is to be decided by the election.

(2) The Prime Minister is able to be toppled if §12 CWR is applicable.

(3) The Prime Minister does not possess political immunity.

 

§2: The Ministers:

(1) Ministers are to be instated by the Prime Minister.

(2) Ministers do not possess political immunity.

(3) New ministers are to be reinstated after every election.

(4) Ministers are able to be toppled if §12 CWR is applicable.

 

§3: The Parliamentarians:

(1) Parliamentarians do not possess political immunity.

(2) Parliamentarians are required to vote for every internal government changes.

(3) This vote shall be performed as stated in the CWE.

 

 

§4 The complete Parliament

(1) The Parliament is not able to:

  1. Pass undemocratic laws

  2. Remove Democracy

  3. Elect the next Prime Minister

(2) The Parliament shall always exist of more than one party as to hinder a dictatorship.

(3) The Parliament shall always respect petitions as stated in §10 CWR

 

 

4. Rules of Partys (CWPR)

(1) Partys are not allowed to spread anti-democratic sentiment

(2) Partys are not allowed to include discriminating material in their campaigns

(3) Partys shall always consist of more that one person

(4) Partys can be dissolved as to defend the state and democracy
